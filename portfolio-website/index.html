About Me

I am Maltiti Azindoo Alidu, an enthusiastic and purpose-driven professional with a background in Agriculture Technology (Biotechnology Option), community development, research, and digital innovation. Born and raised in Tamale, Ghana, with roots in Zabzugu, I bring a deep connection to rural communities and a lifelong commitment to empowering underserved populations through sustainable development, technology, and education.

Since completing my Bachelor of Science degree from the University for Development Studies (UDS) in 2021, I have gathered a diverse portfolio of experiences. My National Service at the Savannah Agricultural Research Institute (CSIR-SARI) sharpened my skills in precision agriculture, laboratory analysis, data collection, and farmer engagement. Since then, I have served in various professional capacities—including roles as an enumerator, scientific technician, software development intern, insurance executive, customer support staff, and community volunteer. I am currently interning with Nobel as part of the AWS re/Start Program and undergoing professional training through multiple fellowships, including the Precision Quality Internship and the Power Learn Project's Software Development Scholarship.

I am passionate about using digital tools and artificial intelligence to solve real-world problems. I leverage AI for research, data analysis, idea generation, and personal learning, which enhances both my academic and professional pursuits. I also founded a small poultry venture, blending agricultural knowledge with entrepreneurial grit—proof of my ability to create solutions that are locally grounded and economically viable.

My strengths lie in data analysis, research, digital literacy, software development, and community facilitation. I have professional certifications in Generative AI, Project Management, Customer Experience, Crop Advisory, and Nonprofit Essentials. I have also studied topics like sustainability, business analysis, and artificial intelligence, which reflect my continuous learning attitude and growth mindset. As a volunteer, I’ve contributed to CHPS compound outreach efforts and rural community education programs. I thrive in collaborative environments, especially those that require adaptation, cultural sensitivity, and solution-oriented thinking. I aspire to a career where I can lead innovative, tech-enabled initiatives that improve lives in rural and underserved communities in Ghana and across Africa.

In the next few years, I see myself becoming a leader in the intersection of agriculture, digital technology, and community development. I am committed to life-long learning, mentorship, and impactful change, and I seek roles that allow me to combine technical expertise with human-centered service.
